Glossary

A

Anime

An anime, also sometimes called japanime or japanimation, refers to an animated series or film from Japan often adapted from a manga.

Anthology

An anthology is a collection of selected texts or pieces sharing the same characteristics: themes, genres, styles, languages, geographical origins, authors, etc. An ancient literary example is the Greek Anthology.

B

Biodiversity

Biodiversity is the living fabric of our planet. It covers all natural environments and life forms (plants, animals, fungi, bacteria, etc.) and their interactions.

C

Polar ice caps

An polar ice cap is a type of glacier that forms a large expanse of continental ice but does not exceed 50,000 km² in area. With a thickness of several hundred metres or even several kilometres, the ice sheet sometimes extends to the sea and forms ice barriers. There are currently two polar ice caps, one in Antarctica (at the South Pole) and one in Greenland (at the North Pole). Because of their position and large surface area, they play a major role in stabilising the Earth's climate.

Comics

Comic is a term used in the United States for comic books. The name comes from the English word "comic" because the first comics published in the United States were humorous. However, in the French-speaking world, its meaning has been limited to referring specifically to American comics.

H

HBO

HBO is an American pay-TV channel that produces and broadcasts series, among other things. Launched on 8 November 1972, HBO is part of the Warner Bros. Discovery group. It has several subsidiaries such as HBO Films and is present in several countries.

J

J-Pop

J-Pop is an abbreviation for Japanese pop.

J-Rock

J-Rock is an abbreviation for Japanese rock.

L

Lights novel

Light novels are Japanese novels that are intended for a young adult audience.

N

Space Shuttle

A space shuttle, in the field of astronautics, conventionally refers to a space vehicle that can return to Earth by making a controlled landing in the manner of an aeroplane or glider and can be reused for a subsequent mission

Nominations

In an artistic or other context, a nomination is a selection, along with a few other people, as a candidate for a prize, award or other honour.

O

Orbit

An orbit is the closed curve representing the path that a celestial object draws in space under the effect of gravitation and inertial forces.

S

Shonen

A shonen is a category of Japanese comics, of the manga type, which is aimed at a male and adolescent readership.

Spin-off

A spin-off is a series derived from the original work, which focuses on secondary characters who are more or less related to the main characters, or on the universe of the work.
